Bach's Fugue in C minor, BWV 847 from The Well-Tempered Clavier Book 1 is a three-voice tonal fugue of 27 bars with a double countersubject. Structure: Enunciation (bars 1-9) presents the subject in the alto voice in C minor, followed by a tonal answer in the treble in G minor, a codetta, and the subject in the bass. The modulatory section (bars 9-26) contains four episodes derived from subject fragments, modulating between C minor, E-flat major, and G minor using sequences and contrary motion. The recapitulatory section (bars 26-31) features final subject entries over a tonic pedal point, with a coda containing independent inner voices, ending on a Tierce de Picardie (C major chord). The subject is a descending scalar motive with rhythmic drive. Both countersubjects accompany every thematic entrance in octave double counterpoint, ensuring harmonic stability amid chromatic tension. The double countersubject allows voice exchange (invertible counterpoint) at the octave throughout.
