Aristotle's Physics establishes the doctrine of four causes: material, formal, efficient, and final. Every natural substance has a telos — an inherent purpose toward which it strives. The acorn's telos is the oak tree. Motion is the actualization of potentiality (energeia). The unmoved mover, as pure actuality without potentiality, drives all celestial motion through final causation. Nature abhors a vacuum; empty space is logically impossible because place is defined by the containing body.
