Vermeer's Girl with a Pearl Earring (c. 1665), oil on canvas, 44.5 × 39 cm, Mauritshuis, The Hague. A tronie (character study) showing a bust-length female figure turning her head toward the viewer. Technique: precise underpainting with white lead mixture applied solidly for bold light-dark patterns, establishing form without preliminary drawings. Soft, diffused light from the left illuminates the face, casting gentle shadows on the right cheek to sculpt three-dimensional realism. Pigments: blues and yellows in the exotic turban (ultramarine and lead-tin yellow), complementing skin tones. The oversized pearl earring is an optical illusion — it lacks contour or hook, created with two highlights (a white spot and a comma-shaped reflection on the collar) that suggest the pearl's form without defining it. Background: featureless dark void (originally a green curtain, now darkened). Composition: close-up view centers the figure, turban frames face, dark background eliminates distractions. Parted lips, direct gaze. No identifiable setting, no narrative context, no symbolic attributes beyond the earring and turban.
