Kandinsky's Composition VII (1913), oil on canvas, 200 × 300 cm, State Tretyakov Gallery, Moscow. Abstract painting featuring a vortex of forms radiating from a central oval pierced by an irregular rectangle. Colors: vibrant contrasting hues — blues, reds, yellows, blacks, and whites — blending and clashing across the surface. Lines: jagged, uneven, and swirling, creating energy and dynamism, intersecting at the center. Shapes: mix of circles, triangles, rectangles, and biomorphic masses forming distinct zones of swirling and geometric elements. Composition achieves harmony through balance despite apparent disorder. No representational forms, though faint suggestions of boats or churches appear in fragments. Created from over 30 preliminary sketches over months; final canvas painted in four days (November 25-28, 1913). Color applied without illusion of real-world depth beyond perceptual recession. The central motif anchors surrounding turmoil. No horizon line, no perspective system, no identifiable space.
