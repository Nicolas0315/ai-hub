In Indian classical music, a raga is not merely a scale but a living entity with characteristic phrases, ornaments called gamaka, and a specific emotional flavor or rasa. The performer explores the raga through alap — a slow, meditative improvisation without rhythmic cycle — before entering the structured tala framework. Each shruti (microtonal interval) carries specific meaning within the raga's grammar. The concept of nada brahma (sound as divine) underlies the entire tradition: music is not entertainment but a path to moksha (liberation).
