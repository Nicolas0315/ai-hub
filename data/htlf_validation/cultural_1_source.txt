The Japanese aesthetic concept of wabi-sabi embraces imperfection and transience. A cracked tea bowl repaired with gold through kintsugi embodies mono no aware — the bittersweet awareness of impermanence. The tea ceremony master demonstrates omotenashi through careful attention to ma, the meaningful emptiness between actions. This sensibility connects to Zen Buddhist concepts of mu (nothingness) and satori (sudden enlightenment), where the practitioner seeks ikigai not through accumulation but through mindful subtraction.
