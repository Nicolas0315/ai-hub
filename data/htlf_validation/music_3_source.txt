Miles Davis's Kind of Blue (1959) employs modal jazz, replacing complex chord progressions with simple scales (modes) and static harmonies. Track analysis: "So What" uses AABA form with A sections on D Dorian (Dm7 chord) and B on E-flat Dorian (Ebm7); a two-chord vamp separates solos. "Freddie Freeloader" is blues-based with tonal variations structured to highlight soloists. "Blue in Green" features unusual scales and static harmony in a contemplative mood. "All Blues" uses blues structure with a flatted sixth over a repeating chord vamp. "Flamenco Sketches" has no head melody; musicians cycle through five predefined scales/modes (including Phrygian) improvising freely on each before advancing. The sessions used sketches rather than full charts; all takes were essentially first takes with no rehearsal. Instrumentation: Miles Davis (trumpet), John Coltrane (tenor sax), Cannonball Adderley (alto sax), Bill Evans (piano), Paul Chambers (bass), Jimmy Cobb (drums). The modal approach emphasizes melodic freedom over harmonic density, slowing the music and stripping complexity compared to bebop.
