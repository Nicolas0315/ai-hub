Picasso's Guernica (1937), oil on canvas, 349.3 × 776.6 cm, Museo Reina Sofía, Madrid. Monochromatic palette: black, white, and gray only. Cubist composition with multiple simultaneous perspectives; compressed, ambiguous space functioning as both interior and exterior. Central triangle structure: a gored, screaming horse pierced by a spear-like wound at center, flanked left by a bull standing over a grieving mother clutching her dead child, and right by a woman at a burning window and a figure holding a lamp. A light bulb with radiant spikes hangs above like an "eye" or bomb flare. A dismembered soldier at bottom holds a broken sword with a small flower. Diagonals accentuate unreal spatial relationships. Angular shapes intersect violently. Newsprint textures visible in some areas. Anatomical dislocations throughout: mouths stretched into screams, eyes displaced, bodies twisted. No depth cues, no conventional perspective, no color. Figures crammed into compressed pictorial space with no traditional focal point.
