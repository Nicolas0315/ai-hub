# Target: Bach Fugue in C minor BWV 847 - Emotional/Critical Description
# URL: https://tonic-chord.com/bach-prelude-and-fugue-no-2-in-c-minor-bwv-847-analysis/
# URL: https://mtosmt.org/issues/mto.12.18.1/mto.12.18.1.barolsky_martens.php

After the prelude's grave, chordal meditation, the fugue erupts with dark, restless energy — like stepping from a cathedral's stillness into a storm-lashed street. The subject descends with urgent purpose, pulling the listener into a vortex of interlocking voices that chase and grapple with one another in ceaseless motion.

There is an almost physical intensity to the way the three voices interweave. Each entry of the subject is a new wave of energy crashing through the texture, the countersubjects wrapping around it like currents in a river. The music conveys a sense of inexorable forward momentum — anxiety made audible, every bar pressing toward the next with breathless urgency.

The episodes offer brief glimpses of light as the music passes through brighter keys, but these moments of relative ease are quickly pulled back into the gravitational field of C minor. The emotional landscape is one of brooding determination — not despair, exactly, but a fierce, concentrated intensity that refuses to relent.

And then, at the very end, the unexpected: a sudden shaft of warm major-key light in the final chord. The Tierce de Picardie arrives like a benediction, transforming the fugue's shadowy turmoil into something luminous and resolved. It is Bach's genius in microcosm — the ability to make mathematical rigor feel like the deepest human emotion.
