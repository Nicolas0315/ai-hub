Modern chemistry explains combustion as rapid oxidation — the combination of a fuel with oxygen, releasing energy as heat and light. Metals oxidize to form metal oxides; smelting reduces these oxides using carbon monoxide from charcoal. The weight gain during oxidation is explained by the addition of oxygen atoms. Priestley's "dephlogisticated air" was actually oxygen gas, which Lavoisier correctly identified as the element consumed during combustion rather than released.
