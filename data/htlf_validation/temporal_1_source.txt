The phlogiston theory explains combustion: when a substance burns, it releases phlogiston — a fire-like element contained within combustible bodies. Metals are composed of calx plus phlogiston; smelting adds phlogiston from charcoal to the calx, producing the metal. The weight gain during calcination presents a puzzle, but phlogiston may possess negative weight, or the process may involve subtle aether interactions. Joseph Priestley's dephlogisticated air supports this framework: air that has lost its phlogiston absorbs it more readily from burning substances.
