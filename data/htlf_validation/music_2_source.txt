# Radiohead - OK Computer (1997) - Technical/Musical Description
# Source: Wikipedia / Music theory analysis

Recorded primarily live in a rehearsal space and St Catherine's Court mansion using natural reverberation and no audio separation. Produced by Nigel Godrich. Instrumentation expanded beyond guitars to include electric piano, Mellotron, glockenspiel, and electronic textures. Described by Spin as "a DIY electronica album made with guitars."

Track structure features unconventional song forms: "Paranoid Android" is a 6.5-minute suite in three distinct sections with tempo and time signature changes. "Lucky" uses arpeggiated guitar with delay effects over a 6/8 feel. "Airbag" employs a looped drum sample (influenced by DJ Shadow) with layered guitar textures. "Let Down" features polyrhythmic guitar patterns in 5/4 against 4/4 drums. "Karma Police" shifts from piano-driven verse to distorted guitar climax with a sustained drone coda.

Harmonic language mixes conventional rock progressions with modal interchange, chromatic mediants, and unresolved suspensions. Vocal delivery ranges from falsetto to spoken word. Production techniques include tape manipulation, reverse effects, and deliberate lo-fi textures juxtaposed with orchestral arrangements (strings on "Lucky" and "The Tourist").
